<p><p>A Ventricular External Drainage (VED) System is a medical device used to manage cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) in patients with conditions like hydrocephalus or intracranial hypertension. It involves placing a catheter into the ventricles of the brain to drain excess fluid, thereby reducing pressure and preventing further neurological complications. The system typically consists of a catheter, valve, and drainage bag, and is utilized mainly in neurosurgical settings.</p><p>The market for Ventricular External Drainage Systems is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing prevalence of neurological disorders and advancements in medical technology.

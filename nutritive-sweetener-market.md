<p><p>The nutritive sweetener market is divided into two main types: artificial and natural sweeteners. Artificial sweeteners are synthetic sugar substitutes, often low in calories, used in a variety of food and beverage products to provide sweetness without the added sugar. In contrast, natural sweeteners are derived from plant sources, such as honey or agave, and may contain calories but are perceived as healthier alternatives. Both types cater to consumers seeking sweetness with different health considerations and dieting preferences.</p></p>

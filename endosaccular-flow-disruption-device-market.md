<p><p>The Endosaccular Flow Disruption Device market primarily features two constructs: cylindrical and spherical. Cylindrical constructs are designed to fit within the target aneurysm, providing targeted flow disruption while maintaining vessel access. In contrast, spherical constructs create a broader, uniform occlusion by expanding within the aneurysm sac. Both designs aim to promote thrombosis and prevent blood flow to the aneurysm, ultimately improving patient outcomes in endovascular procedures. Their unique shapes cater to different anatomical and clinical needs in the treatment of vascular conditions.</p></p>
